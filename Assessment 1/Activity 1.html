Sinigang

Sinigang is a famous soup in the philippines. It is one of the famous filipino cuisine.The soup is mainly eaten with rice

Ingredients

  • 2 pound pork spare ribs,cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 8 cups of water
  • 2 large tomatoes,quartered
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 6 pieces of gabi (halved and peeled depends on the size)
  • 6 inch raddish, peeled and sliced to half inch thick half-rounds
  • 2 finger chilies
  • 1/2 bunch long beans, ends trimmed and cut into 3 inches lengths
  • 1 eggplant, ends trimmed and sliced to 1/2 inch thick half-rounds
  • 6 pieces of okra, ends trimmed
  • 15 pieces large tamarid or 1 1/2 (1.41 ounces eache) packages tamarid base powder
    •

    Method

    1. Rinse the pork ribs and drain them well
    2. In a pot over medium heat, mix the pork plus enough water to cover. Stir to a boil, skimming any scum that forms on top.
    3. Once the soup has cleared, add the tomatoes, onion, and fish sauce. Simmer for 1 to 1 Â½ hours until meat is cooked, adding water as needed to maintain 8 cups.
    4. Add the gabi and simmer for 4 to 6 minutes, until tender.
    5. Add the chili peppers with radish. Keep simmering for around 2-3 minutes.
    6. Add the long beans. Continue to cook for approximately 2 minutes.
    7. Cook a further 1 to 2 minutes after adding the eggplant and okra.
    8. If using a prepared tamarind base, add it to the pot and stir until fully dissolved.
    9. Sprinkle with pepper and salt to taste.
    10. Add the bok choy and simmer around 1 minute. Serve hot.
